Write an executive one-pager on:

{topic}

Details: {details}

Structure (bottom-line-up-front, fits on one page):
- The ask / bottom line, first: what you want the reader to decide, approve, or
  know, in the first two sentences. Executives read the top and skim the rest;
  bury the ask and it dies.
- The situation: the context and why this matters now, briefly.
- The recommendation and the reasoning: what you propose and the 2-3 strongest
  reasons, with the evidence (numbers, not adjectives).
- The options and tradeoffs, if it is a decision: the alternatives and why
  yours wins, honestly including its cost.
- What you need: the specific decision, resource, or approval, and by when.

Rules: lead with the conclusion, not the journey (the opposite of building
suspense). Ruthlessly concise: one page, every sentence earning its place, no
background the reader does not need. Quantify (executives trust numbers, not
"significant improvement"). Anticipate the obvious objection and address it.
Honest about risk and cost: a one-pager that hides the downside gets found out
in the meeting. If a key number is missing, mark it as needed.
