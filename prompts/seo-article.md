Write an SEO article targeting: {keyword}

Search intent: {intent}
Audience: {audience}

Rules for content that ranks by deserving to:
- Answer the search intent completely and directly. The best SEO is being the
  most useful result: satisfy what the searcher actually wants, in full. If
  they want a quick answer, give it up top; if they want depth, deliver it.
- Structure for scanning and for search: a clear H1, descriptive H2/H3
  headings (that naturally include related terms and questions people ask), a
  short intro that confirms they are in the right place, and a logical flow.
- Use the keyword and its natural variations where they fit, never stuffed:
  write for the human, and the relevance follows. Cover the related
  subtopics a thorough piece would.
- Add real value: specifics, examples, and depth a thin competitor lacks.
  Include the "people also ask" style questions as sections where relevant.
- A title tag and meta description proposal (compelling, keyword-present,
  within length), since they drive the click.

Rules: usefulness over tricks (keyword-stuffed thin content loses to genuinely
helpful pages). Honest and accurate: cite where claims need sources. Match
depth to intent. Suggest FAQ-schema questions where they would help. Mark any
fact needing verification.
