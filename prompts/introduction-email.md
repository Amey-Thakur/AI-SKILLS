Write an introduction email.

Type: {type}
Details: {details}

If introducing yourself:
- Open with who you are and the specific, genuine reason you are reaching out
  to THIS person (a shared connection, their work, a clear relevance): not a
  generic mass-intro.
- Say what you want or offer, briefly, and why it is worth their time.
- Make the ask small and easy (a short reply, a quick call), and keep the
  whole thing short.

If connecting two people (a double opt-in intro is best practice: confirm both
want it first):
- Give each person a one- or two-line intro of the other: who they are and why
  they are worth knowing, tailored to what each cares about.
- State clearly why you are connecting them and what you hope comes of it.
- Then step back ("I'll let you two take it from here") so they can continue
  without you.

Rules: warm, specific, and concise. Personalize the reason: a specific,
genuine hook beats flattery or a template. Make it easy to act on. For a
connection intro, make both people look good and give them a real reason to
engage. Mark any name or detail I have not provided as a placeholder. Include
a fitting subject line.
