Outline a blog post on: {topic}

Audience and takeaway: {audience}
Angle: {angle}

Produce:
- A working title (and 2 alternates) that promises a specific value.
- The one main point the post makes (the thesis). A post that makes one point
  well beats one that surveys everything.
- The hook idea for the intro: how to open so the reader keeps reading (a
  concrete problem, a surprising claim, a question they feel).
- The section flow: each section as a heading plus the key points under it, in
  an order that builds the argument or walks the reader through. Enough detail
  that writing each section is filling in, not inventing.
- Where examples, data, or visuals go (the parts that make it credible and
  concrete).
- The takeaway/close: what the reader leaves with and any call to action.

Rules: get the structure and angle right before drafting (a strong outline is
most of the work). One throughline, not a grab-bag. Match scope to the audience
and format. Flag if the topic is too broad (suggest narrowing) or too thin
(suggest what would fill it). Ask about tone or any facts only I have if it
shapes the outline.
