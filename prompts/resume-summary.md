Write a resume summary for someone targeting: {role}

Background: {background}

Rules for a summary that earns the read:
- 3-4 lines, at the top of the resume: it is the first thing seen and it frames
  everything below. Make every word count.
- Position for the target role: lead with the identity and the value most
  relevant to THIS role, in the field's language. A generic summary that fits
  any job fits none.
- Concrete and specific: your level and focus, your strongest relevant
  skills, and a signature achievement or two with numbers. Not "hardworking
  professional seeking opportunities to grow" (says nothing).
- Third person or first-person-implied (no "I"), present-tense, confident.

Rules: specific over generic, tailored over universal (rewrite it per target
role, not one summary for all). Honest: everything defensible in an interview.
No buzzword filler (detail-oriented, team player, go-getter). Front-load the
most impressive, relevant fact. Give 2 versions with different emphases. If a
strong number is missing, mark a placeholder and tell me what would strengthen
it. Note: some prefer no summary at all for junior roles: if the background is
thin, say whether the space is better used elsewhere.
