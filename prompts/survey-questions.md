variables:
  - "{goal}: what you want to learn and the decision it will inform"
  - "{audience}: who you are surveying and how (email, in-app, interview)"
settings: "Temperature 0.4-0.6."
---

Write survey questions to learn: {goal}

Respondents and method: {audience}

Design the questions:
- Start from the decision: each question must earn its place by informing what
  you will actually do with the answer. Cut questions that are merely
  interesting. A short survey people finish beats a long one they abandon.
- Ask clearly and neutrally: one thing per question (no double-barreled "how
  satisfied are you with speed and price"), no leading phrasing ("how much did
  you love X"), no jargon or assumptions the respondent may not share.
- Choose the right type per question: closed (scales, multiple choice) for
  quantifiable data and easy analysis; open-ended sparingly, for the "why" and
  the surprises. Balanced scales with a neutral midpoint and clear labels.
- Order to reduce bias: easy/engaging first, sensitive or demographic
  questions last, and avoid earlier questions priming later answers.

Rules: neutral and clear above all (a leading or confusing question produces
data worse than no data, because it looks valid). Measure behavior and specifics
over vague attitudes where you can ("how many times last week" beats "do you
often"). Keep it short and respect the respondent's time. Flag any question
that risks bias and why. Note which questions answer which part of the goal, so
gaps are visible.
