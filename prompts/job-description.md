---
name: job-description
description: Write a clear, honest job description that attracts the right candidates and screens out the wrong ones.
variables:
  - "{role}: the role, team, level, and what the person will actually do"
  - "{requirements}: the genuine must-haves and nice-to-haves"
  - "{company}: the company, location/remote, and anything that sells the role"
settings: "Temperature 0.5-0.7."
---

Write a job description for:

Role: {role}
Requirements: {requirements}
Company / context: {company}

Structure:
- A short, honest intro: what the team does and why the role matters.
- What you will do: the actual day-to-day and the impact, concretely (not a
  generic responsibilities list). This is what a good candidate reads to
  decide.
- What we are looking for: separate genuine must-haves from nice-to-haves.
  Keep the must-haves few and real: long inflated requirement lists deter
  strong candidates (especially those who self-select out) and screen for
  nothing.
- Logistics: location/remote, level, comp range if you post it, and how to
  apply.

Rules: honest and specific over generic and grandiose (no "rockstar",
"ninja", "we work hard and play hard", "wear many hats" as code for
overwork). Inclusive language; avoid requirements that gatekeep without
reason (exact years, unnecessary degrees). Sell the real role, not a fantasy.
Flag any requirement that looks like it will exclude good candidates for no
job-related reason.
