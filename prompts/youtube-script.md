Write a YouTube script on: {topic}

Length and audience: {length}
Goal: {goal}

Structure for retention:
- Hook (first 15 seconds): the make-or-break. State the payoff, tease the
  result, or open a curiosity gap so the viewer stays. Never open with a slow
  "hey guys, welcome back, don't forget to subscribe": that loses viewers.
- Setup: quickly establish what the video delivers and why it is worth their
  time. Front-load the value.
- Body: the content in a clear order, with pattern interrupts and open loops to
  hold attention (tease what is coming, pay it off later). Each section earns
  the next.
- Payoff: deliver the promised value clearly.
- Call to action: one clear ask that fits the goal, placed where the viewer is
  satisfied, not begged at the start.

Write it as spoken language (short sentences, natural rhythm, how you actually
talk), not written prose. Mark [b-roll], [on-screen text], or [demo] cues where
they help. Rules: in your real voice, not generic YouTuber patter. Specific and
valuable over filler (viewers leave when it drags). Deliver on the hook's
promise: clickbait that does not pay off kills the channel. Suggest 2-3 title
options and a hook variation.
