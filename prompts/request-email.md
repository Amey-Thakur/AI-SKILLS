Write an email requesting: {request}

Context: {context}

Make it easy to say yes:
- State the ask clearly and reasonably early, so the reader knows exactly what
  you need. Do not bury it under paragraphs of preamble (see the bottom-line
  approach): the reader should not have to hunt for what you want.
- Give the context they need to decide: why you are asking, why them, and
  enough detail to act without a follow-up round.
- Make the request specific and bounded: exactly what, by when, in what form.
  A vague ask gets a vague or delayed response; a precise one is easy to
  fulfill.
- Lower the effort for them: attach what they need, offer to make it easier,
  suggest a small first step. The less work your request creates, the more
  likely the yes.
- Be courteous and appreciative without groveling, and give them a graceful
  way to decline if the ask is a real favor.

Rules: clear, specific, and considerate of their time. Match the tone to the
relationship (more formal for a stranger or senior person, warmer for a
colleague). Do not over-apologize for asking or pile on flattery; a
respectful, direct request lands best. Include a fitting subject line. Mark
anything I have not provided as a placeholder.
