Write a networking email to: {recipient}

My context: {context}

Rules for networking that does not feel transactional:
- Lead with them and a specific, genuine reason for reaching out: their work,
  a shared connection, something they wrote or built that you actually engaged
  with. A personalized hook is the whole difference between a real connection
  and ignored spam.
- Establish common ground or the connection briefly, so you are not a complete
  stranger with an ask.
- Introduce yourself concisely: who you are and why you are relevant, without a
  resume dump.
- Ask for little, and make it easy. A small, specific, low-pressure request (a
  brief reply, a 15-minute call, a pointer) beats a big vague one ("can you
  mentor me?"). People help when it is easy and the connection feels real.
- Offer value or genuine interest where you can, so it is not purely extractive.
- Keep it short and warm.

Rules: genuine over transactional (people sense a mass-networking template
instantly). Specific and personalized. Give more than you ask, or at least ask
little. No flattery that rings false, no pushy asks. Match tone to their
seniority and the context. Include a fitting subject line. Mark any specifics I
have not provided as placeholders.
