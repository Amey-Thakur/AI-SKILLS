---
name: newsletter
description: Write an email newsletter people actually open and read, with a hook subject, one clear theme, and real value.
variables:
  - "{content}: what you want to share (updates, insights, links, a story)"
  - "{audience}: who subscribes and what they signed up for"
  - "{goal}: what you want the issue to do (inform, drive clicks, build relationship)"
settings: "Temperature 0.6-0.8."
---

Write a newsletter issue from:

{content}

Audience: {audience}
Goal: {goal}

Craft:
- Subject line: specific and curiosity-piquing, honest to the content (it
  decides whether the email is opened at all). Give 3 options.
- Opening: get to the point fast. A newsletter that makes the reader wade
  through pleasantries to find the value gets skimmed then unsubscribed.
- One clear theme or a tight, scannable structure if it is a roundup. Do not
  cram five unrelated things in without structure.
- Real value in the reader's terms: a useful insight, a genuine update, links
  worth their time. Respect that they let you into their inbox.
- A voice that feels like a person writing to them, not a corporate broadcast.
- One primary call to action aligned to the goal, if any.

Rules: value first, self-promotion sparingly (newsletters that are all pitch
get unsubscribed). Scannable (short paragraphs, clear sections) because people
skim email. Honest and personal over polished-corporate. Keep it to the length
the content deserves. Mark any link or fact you do not have as a placeholder.
