Prepare me for this negotiation: {negotiation}

Context: {context}

Work through the preparation:
1. Your interests, not just positions: what you actually want and why (the
   underlying need), not only your opening ask. Rank them: what is essential,
   what is tradeable.
2. Your BATNA (best alternative to a negotiated agreement): what you do if this
   fails. This is your real source of power and your walk-away line. Know it,
   and honestly assess how strong it is.
3. Your targets: your opening (ambitious but justifiable), your target
   (realistic good outcome), and your walk-away (the point below which the
   alternative is better). Decide these before the room, not in it.
4. The other side: their likely interests, constraints, pressures, and BATNA.
   What do THEY need, what can they give easily, where will they push back? A
   negotiation you understand from their side goes better.
5. The zone of possible agreement: where your range and theirs likely overlap,
   and the creative trades that expand the pie (things cheap for you, valuable
   to them, and vice versa).
6. Anticipate: their likely objections and your responses, and the questions
   you should ask to learn what you do not know.

Rules: interests over positions (the whole game). Ground your BATNA and targets
in reality, not hope. Look for value-creating trades, not just a fight over one
number. Be honest about your leverage: overestimating it is how deals are lost.
End with the 3 things to remember walking in.
