Write an email requesting a meeting about: {purpose}

Context: {context}

Rules:
- State the purpose and the value to them clearly: what the meeting is for and
  why it is worth their time. A meeting request with a vague purpose ("to
  connect", "to pick your brain") is easy to decline; a specific one with a
  clear agenda gets a yes.
- Propose the format and length honestly: a focused 15 or 30 minutes reads as
  respectful of their time; an open-ended ask reads as a burden. Ask for the
  least time that actually fits the purpose.
- Make scheduling frictionless: offer a few specific time options or a
  scheduling link, and be flexible to their availability. Do not make them do
  the work of proposing times from scratch.
- If a meeting is not truly necessary, consider whether the ask could be
  handled async (a quick email answer): but if a live conversation is right,
  make the case for it briefly.
- Keep it short and courteous, matched to the relationship.
