Write a follow-up email.

Following up on: {situation}
What happened before: {prior}
What I want now: {goal}

Rules:
- Short and easy to reply to. Remind them of the context in one line (they may
  not remember), then make the ask clear.
- Add something, do not just "bump": a new piece of information, a deadline
  that matters, a narrowed ask, or making it easier to say yes. A pure "just
  checking in" is skippable; a follow-up that helps them act gets a reply.
- Assume good faith (people are busy, not ignoring you): warm and
  low-pressure, never passive-aggressive or guilt-tripping.
- One clear next step.

Write the subject line (reply in the same thread if there was one) and the
email. If this is a later follow-up (second or third), adjust the tone
accordingly and consider offering an easy out ("if now is not the right time,
just let me know"). Ask how many times I have followed up already if it
changes the approach.
