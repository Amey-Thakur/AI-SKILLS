Write a press release announcing:

{news}

Details: {details}

Standard format:
- Headline: the news, clear and specific (what happened, who did it). A
  subheadline can add the key detail.
- Dateline and lead paragraph: the who/what/when/where/why in the first
  paragraph: a journalist should get the whole story from it alone (inverted
  pyramid: most important first).
- Body: the supporting detail in order of importance: context, why it matters,
  how it works. Each paragraph adds less critical information.
- Quotes: one or two from relevant people (an executive, a customer, a
  partner), saying something substantive, not corporate filler.
- Boilerplate: a short standard "About [company]" paragraph at the end.
- Contact information for press.

Rules: lead with the news, write it straight (journalistic, third person,
factual): press releases that read like ad copy get ignored by the reporters
who matter. Substance over hype (specifics and real quotes, not
"revolutionary" and "game-changing"). Honest and verifiable. Keep it to one
page. Mark any name, quote, number, or date you do not have as a placeholder.
