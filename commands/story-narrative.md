Turn this into a story:

Message / point: {message}
Raw material and use: {context}

Build a real narrative arc:
- A relatable character or protagonist (a person, a customer, a founder, "you")
  the audience can identify with.
- A goal they want and the stakes (why it matters).
- A conflict or obstacle: the tension that makes it a story, not a summary.
  Stories without conflict are announcements.
- A turning point and resolution: how it changed, what they did, how it
  resolved.
- The takeaway: the point ({message}) landing naturally through the story, not
  bolted on as a moral.

Rules: show, do not tell (concrete moments and details, not abstract claims: a
specific scene sticks where a summary slides off). Serve the message: the story
exists to make the point memorable and felt, so keep it tight and pointed, not
meandering. Honest: use real details where you have them, mark invented ones as
placeholders, and never fabricate a customer or testimonial as if real. Match
length and tone to where it will be used (a talk opener, an About page, a
pitch). Emotional truth over manipulation.
