Outline: {topic}

Audience and goal: {audience}

Build the outline:
1. Define the one thing the reader/learner should be able to do or understand
   by the end. Everything in the outline serves it; cut what does not.
2. Structure the flow logically: for teaching, order by dependency (each part
   builds on the last, no forward references). For persuasion, order by the
   argument. For a guide, order by the reader's journey.
3. Break into sections with a clear purpose each, and under each, the key
   points or subsections. Enough detail that writing is filling in, not
   figuring out.
4. Note where examples, visuals, exercises, or evidence go: these are where
   content earns its keep, so plan them in.
5. Check the shape: a strong opening (hook and promise), a body that delivers
   in a sensible order, and a close that lands the takeaway.

Rules: nail the structure before writing (a good outline makes the writing
easy; a bad one makes it a slog you rewrite). One clear throughline, not a
survey of everything. Right-size the scope to the format and the audience's
attention. Flag where the topic is too broad and should be split, or too thin
and should be combined. Ask what depth or angle if it changes the outline.
