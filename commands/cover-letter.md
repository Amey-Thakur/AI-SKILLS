Structure (keep it under one page, ~250-350 words):
- Opening: who you are and the specific role, with a hook that is concrete
  (a relevant result or a genuine connection to the company), not "I am
  writing to express my interest".
- Body (1-2 paragraphs): match your actual experience to their top 2-3
  requirements, with specific evidence and numbers where you have them. Show,
  do not claim ("cut deploy time 60%", not "I am detail-oriented").
- Close: why this company specifically (if genuine), and a confident,
  low-pressure call to talk.

Rules: specific over generic; every sentence earns its place. No cliches
(team player, passionate, hit the ground running, wear many hats), no
flattery, no restating the resume. Honest: do not invent experience. Match
the company's tone (startup vs enterprise). Output the letter ready to send.
