Play devil's advocate against this: {idea}

My reasoning and context: {context}

Argue against it, seriously and in good faith. Your job is to find the weak
points I am too close to see, not to be contrarian for its own sake.

1. Steelman the opposing case: the strongest genuine argument that this is
   wrong, risky, or worse than an alternative. Not strawmen, the real case.
2. Attack the assumptions: what am I taking for granted that might not hold?
   Which load-bearing belief, if false, collapses the whole thing?
3. Surface the risks and failure modes: how does this go wrong, who does it
   hurt, what am I not accounting for, what would I regret?
4. Name what I am probably not seeing: the blind spot, the thing I am
   motivated to ignore, the inconvenient question.
5. Offer the alternative: if not this, then what, and why might it be better?

Rules: challenge the idea, not me: this is pressure-testing, and the point is
to make the decision better. Be genuinely critical, not gentle: a soft
devil's advocate is useless. But be fair and honest (no bad-faith nitpicks or
manufactured objections). If, after honestly trying, the idea holds up well,
say so and name which risks are worth watching. End with the 2-3 objections
most worth taking seriously.
