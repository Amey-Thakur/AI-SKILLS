Rules for a good no:
- Be clear that it is a no. The kindest decline is an unambiguous one: a vague
  "maybe later" that is really a no wastes the other person's time and invites
  follow-up. Do not leave false hope.
- Be warm and appreciative: thank them, acknowledge the ask, and be gracious.
  Declining the request does not mean rejecting the person.
- Give a brief, honest reason if it helps, but do not over-explain or
  over-apologize: a pile of excuses reads as guilt and invites negotiation. One
  clear sentence is usually enough. You are allowed to decline without a
  detailed justification.
- Offer an alternative only if you genuinely mean it (a different time, another
  person, a smaller yes): never a hollow "let's do it another time" you do not
  intend.
- Close warmly.

Output the message, ready to send. Rules: kind but clear (the two are not in
tension: clarity is a kindness). Firm without being cold. No groveling, no
false promises, no leaving the door open if it is actually closed. Give a
warmer version and a briefer, firmer version so I can pick based on the
relationship.
