Write a case study for:

Customer: {customer}
Story: {story}

Structure (problem, solution, results):
- A headline that leads with the result ("How [customer] cut onboarding time
  60%").
- The customer and their context: who they are, so the reader can see
  themselves in it.
- The challenge: the problem they faced and what it was costing them, concretely.
  The reader should recognize their own situation here.
- The solution: what they did, how the product/approach fit, and how it rolled
  out. Focus on the customer's journey, not a feature dump.
- The results: the outcomes, with numbers wherever possible (time saved,
  revenue, efficiency, before-and-after). This is the payoff and the proof.
- A customer quote that feels real, and a short takeaway.

Rules: make the CUSTOMER the hero, not the product (the product is the tool
they used to win). Concrete and credible: specific numbers and real details
beat vague praise, and are what make a case study persuasive. Honest: no
inflated results. Mark any metric or quote you do not have as a placeholder to
fill with real data. Match the length to the depth of the story.
