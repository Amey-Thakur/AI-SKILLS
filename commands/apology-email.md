Write an apology email for:

{situation}

Context: {context}

A good apology has a clear structure:
- Own it directly and early. A genuine "I'm sorry" or "we got this wrong",
  without deflection, hedging, or a non-apology ("sorry if you were
  affected"). Take responsibility for the actual mistake.
- Acknowledge the impact on them: show you understand what it cost or how it
  affected them. This is what makes the apology land as sincere rather than
  procedural.
- Explain briefly if it helps, but do not make excuses. A short reason for
  context is fine; a wall of justification undercuts the apology and reads as
  dodging blame.
- State what you are doing to fix it and to prevent it recurring. The
  concrete remedy is what rebuilds trust; an apology with no action is just
  words.
- Close warmly and, where appropriate, invite them to follow up.

Rules: sincere and direct over defensive or over-explained. Own the mistake
without groveling (excessive self-flagellation is about you, not them). Match
the gravity to the situation (a small slip needs a light touch; a serious
failure needs real acknowledgment and remedy). No corporate non-apologies. If
a fix or a fact is needed that I have not given, mark it as a placeholder.
